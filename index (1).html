About the lab

Based in the Department of Chemical & Petroleum Engineering at United Arab Emirates University, Alsuwaidi's ChemEng·AI Lab works at the seam between classical chemical reaction engineering and modern computational methods. Our central question is simple to state and hard to answer: what really happens inside an industrial reactor, and can we predict it well enough to control it?

Much of our work centers on fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) regenerators — the units where spent catalyst is restored by burning off coke. We study coke oxidation under conventional and oxy-fuel conditions, combining computational fluid dynamics with bench-scale experiments to map temperature, flow, and reaction fields that are otherwise invisible. This work has been carried out in close collaboration with industry, including Sinopec.

Increasingly, we let machine learning carry the load. By pairing convolutional autoencoders with data-driven models, we build digital twins that predict full reactor temperature fields in a fraction of the time a full simulation would take — turning hours of computation into near-instant prediction.

The lab's research direction is shaped by training under Prof. Lian Zhang and Prof. Shibo Kuang, whose work spans clean solid-fuel technologies, combustion, and computational process engineering. The group's roots also reach into materials: hierarchical metal–organic frameworks and their pore engineering for selective adsorption and separation.

Research themes

/ 01

Catalyst regeneration & combustion

Coke oxidation in FCC regenerators under O₂/N₂ and O₂/CO₂ atmospheres, combining CFD with experimental analysis to understand and optimize regeneration.

/ 02

AI-driven digital twins

Convolutional autoencoder dimensionality reduction plus machine learning to predict reactor temperature fields fast enough for real-time, twin-based operation.

/ 03

Functional materials & separation

Hierarchical MOF pore engineering and finned, cellulose-supported frameworks for the selective adsorption of close-boiling hydrocarbons.
